How RBK Screener Works

RBK Dynamic Market Screener tracks all NSE equity stocks using real bhavcopy data you upload, and scores each stock from three different angles. You can use any one — or all three — depending on your investment style.

🚀 Momentum Score

Based entirely on price action and technical indicators. Finds stocks with strong short-term momentum. Best for short-term trading (days to weeks). Works without any fundamental data.

🚀 Momentum Score (0–100)

The Momentum Score tracks 13 technical factors from your NSE bhavcopy price data. The score peaks when multiple signals align: the stock is rising, volume confirms the move, it's outperforming the market, and it hasn't yet become overextended.

ScoreVerdictWhat to expect
65–100BUYStrong technical alignment — multiple signals positive
40–64HOLDMixed signals — no clear direction
0–39AVOIDWeak or negative momentum — trend is against you

The 13 Factors

5-Day Price Momentummax 12 pts
How much has the price moved in the last 5 sessions (one week)? Higher positive % = more points. Negative momentum scores 0 (not penalised, since dips can be normal in an uptrend).
10-Day Price Momentummax 8 pts
Same concept over 2 weeks. Confirms that the short-term move isn't just a one-day spike. Only positive momentum earns points here.
Price vs 20-Day Moving Averagemax 10 pts
Is the stock trading above its 20-day average? If yes = uptrend confirmed. All or nothing — 10 points if above, 0 if below.
20-Day Average Directionmax 8 pts
Is the average itself rising? A rising 20-day average means the trend is broad-based, not just a single-session spike.
Relative Strength Index — RSI (14-day)max 12 pts, can go negative
RSI measures speed and magnitude of recent price changes (0–100). Sweet spot for momentum: 55–65. Above 80 = overbought and gets penalised (sine-wave pattern — extremes tend to revert). Below 25 = also penalised (extreme oversold). This is different from Long Term where low RSI is desirable.
Volume Surge vs 20-Day Averagemax 8 pts
Is today's volume unusually high? Volume is the "fuel" behind price moves. A stock rising on 2x its normal volume is much more convincing than one rising on low volume.
Relative Strength vs Broader Marketmax 14 pts
Is this stock outperforming the overall market? Built from an equal-weighted index of all 2,400+ stocks we track. Separates leaders from stocks just rising with the tide.
MACD (12,26,9)max 12 pts
Moving Average Convergence Divergence. When the MACD line crosses above its signal line with an accelerating histogram = bullish crossover = maximum points.
Bollinger Band Positionmax 8 pts
Where is the price within its 20-day volatility band? 50–95% of the band = healthy momentum zone. Above 95% = overextended, reduced points.
ADX Trend Strengthmax 8 pts
Average Directional Index — measures how strong the trend is (not its direction). Above 25 with +DI > −DI = confirmed strong uptrend.
Golden Cross / Death Crossmax 8 pts
50-day vs 200-day moving average. When the 50-day crosses above the 200-day (Golden Cross) = long-term bullish signal. Death Cross (opposite) = 0 points.
52-Week High Proximitymax 6 pts
How close is the stock to its annual high? Stocks near their 52-week highs are in proven sustained uptrends. Far below = sustained weakness.
Price Gap Detectionmax 6 pts
Did the stock open significantly above yesterday's close? A gap up signals strong overnight buying conviction. Gap down = 0 points.

🏦 Fundamental Score (0–100)

The Fundamental Score measures the financial health and valuation of the business itself — independent of what the stock price is doing day-to-day. A company can have strong fundamentals but weak momentum (temporarily beaten down), or strong momentum but weak fundamentals (hype-driven).

This score only appears when you've uploaded XBRL Excel files for a company to docs/data/xbrl/ in Git. The GitHub Action automatically generates fundamentals.csv from these files.
ScoreVerdictMeaning
65–100STRONGBusiness is financially solid — profitable, low debt, good margins
40–64FAIRAverage — some positives, some concerns
0–39WEAKFinancial concerns — low profitability, high debt, or very expensive

The 5 Fundamental Factors

⚡ All 5 factors are benchmarked against the company's own sector — not a universal standard. A D/E of 2.0 is perfectly normal for a bank but alarming for an IT company.
Return on Equity (ROE)max 25 pts
How profitably does the company use shareholders' money? Formula: Net Profit ÷ Shareholders' Equity × 100. Compared against the sector's typical minimum ROE. IT sector expects 18%+, banks expect 12%+, infrastructure might be fine at 8%.
Debt to Equity Ratiomax 20 pts
How much debt does the company carry relative to its equity? Judged against the sector norm — banks legitimately have high D/E (loans are debt), FMCG companies should have near-zero. A D/E that's 25% of the sector's typical maximum earns full points.
Net Profit Marginmax 20 pts
How much of every ₹1 in revenue becomes actual profit? Compared against the sector range. Textiles might normally achieve 3–10%, while IT companies typically achieve 15–30%. Being above your sector norm earns more points than being above a universal threshold.
Current Ratio (Liquidity)max 15 pts
Can the company pay its short-term bills? Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities. Above 2 = very comfortable. Below 1 = may struggle (with a special exception for banks/NBFCs where this naturally reads low).
P/E Ratio (live)max 20 pts
Price-to-Earnings = today's price ÷ annual EPS. Computed live so it stays accurate as the price moves. Compared against the sector's typical P/E range — an IT company at P/E 25 is fair, while a PSU bank at P/E 25 would be expensive. The sector context changes everything.

Sector detection

The app reads the DescriptionOfSingleSegment field from your XBRL filing (the company's own description of their business) and maps it to 12 predefined sectors: Banking & Finance, IT & Software, FMCG & Consumer, Pharma & Healthcare, Automobile, Infrastructure, Chemicals, Metals & Mining, Power & Energy, Textiles, Real Estate, Telecom. A "General" category is used as fallback.

You can verify the detected sector by opening any stock and expanding the Fundamental Score Breakdown — it always shows which sector was used at the bottom of the breakdown.

🏆 Long Term Score

The Long Term tab is based on a completely different philosophy from Momentum. Understanding the difference is key:

🚀 Momentum🏆 Long Term
StrategyBuy what is already risingBuy quality at a good entry point
RSI 30 (oversold)⚠️ Penalised✅ Best entry signal
RSI 65 (rising)✅ Ideal⚠️ Less attractive entry
Time horizonDays to weeksWeeks to months
Needs fundamentals?NoYes — essential
RiskTrend reversalValue trap (dip keeps falling)

How the Long Term Score is calculated

1. ROE vs Sector Benchmarkmax 30 pts
Is the company genuinely profitable for its sector? A company well above its sector's typical ROE earns full points — this is the single biggest driver of the Long Term score.
2. Debt Safetymax 20 pts
Can the company survive a prolonged downturn? Low debt relative to sector norms means the company isn't at risk of a debt spiral if revenue drops temporarily.
3. Profit Margin vs Sectormax 15 pts
Margins above the sector norm suggest a genuine business quality advantage — pricing power, efficiency, or brand value.
4. Entry Point (RSI)max 20 pts
This is the opposite of Momentum. RSI below 30 (heavily oversold) earns the most points — it signals that the stock is temporarily beaten down, which is the best time to enter a quality company. RSI above 60 earns very little — the stock is already running, so you're buying in late.
5. Valuation vs Sector P/Emax 15 pts
Is the stock trading below what's typical for its sector? A stock at P/E well below its sector's normal range means you're buying a quality business cheaply — the classic "margin of safety."
💡 The best Long Term candidates combine: high ROE + low debt + good margins (factors 1–3) AND are currently oversold (RSI below 40) AND trading below their sector's typical P/E (factor 5). All five aligning is rare — but when they do, it's typically a genuinely compelling opportunity.
⚠️ A low RSI alone is never sufficient. Many stocks with RSI below 30 are there because their business is deteriorating. The Long Term tab only shows stocks where both the RSI is low AND the fundamentals are strong. Without uploaded XBRL data, a stock won't appear here at all.

🔍 Patterns & Signals

Open any stock and click the 🔍 Patterns tab to see detected candlestick patterns from the last 2 sessions.

Candlestick Patterns

🟢 Bullish Engulfing

A large green candle completely covers a red candle from the previous day. Bulls completely overpowered bears in one session. Strongest when it appears after a sustained downtrend with high volume.

🔴 Bearish Engulfing

A large red candle completely covers the previous green candle. Strong reversal signal — sellers took over in a single session. Most reliable near a recent price peak.

🟡 Doji

Open and close are nearly identical, forming a cross shape. Indecision — neither buyers nor sellers won. Often signals a pause or reversal in the current trend.

🟢 Hammer

Small body at the top, long lower tail (at least 2× the body). Sellers pushed price down hard but buyers recovered it. Suggests selling exhaustion, especially after a downtrend.

🔴 Shooting Star

Small body at the bottom, long upper tail. Buyers pushed price up sharply but sellers brought it back down. Buying enthusiasm fading, often near a price peak.

⚪ Inside Bar

Today's entire range sits inside yesterday's range. Market is coiling — neither side making progress. Often precedes a sharp breakout. Watch for a break above/below today's range.

🟢 Gap Up / 🔴 Gap Down

Stock opened significantly above (gap up) or below (gap down) yesterday's close. Gaps represent overnight shifts in supply/demand — often continuation signals when volume confirms them.

Valuation Badges

Shown on stock cards when fundamental data is available. Always relative to the company's own sector:

UNDERVALUED — P/E below sector norm, and/or Price-to-Book below 1.5, and strong fundamentals. Trading at a discount to what's typical for this type of company.

NEUTRAL — Mixed signals. Stock is fairly priced for its sector — not a bargain, not expensive.

OVERVALUED — P/E significantly above sector norm. Significant optimism already priced in. Doesn't mean it won't go higher, but the margin of safety is low.

💡 Hover over any valuation badge on the stock card to see which sector's benchmarks were used to compute it.

What's on the chart automatically

  • Yellow line — SMA 20: 20-day Simple Moving Average. Short-term trend reference. Price above it = uptrend.
  • Purple line — SMA 50: 50-day average. Medium-term trend. Appears when enough history exists.
  • Green dashed lines (S1, S2, S3): Support zones — swing lows where buyers have previously stepped in. These are "buyer zones."
  • Red dashed lines (R1, R2, R3): Resistance zones — swing highs where sellers have previously appeared. These are "seller zones."
  • Volume bars at the bottom: Green = up-day volume, red = down-day volume.

Pivot Points (🎯 button)

Classic daily pivot calculations based on the previous session's High/Low/Close. Seven levels: Pivot (blue), R1/R2/R3 (resistance, in yellow to red), S1/S2/S3 (support, in yellow to green). "Buy at support, sell at resistance" is the core idea.

Candlestick basics

  • Green candle — Close was above Open (buyers won the day)
  • Red candle — Close was below Open (sellers won the day)
  • Upper wick — Highest price reached during the session
  • Lower wick — Lowest price reached during the session
  • Wide body — Strong decisive session
  • Long wicks — Price rejected at extremes — important levels

Price filter

Filters by current price: <100 / 100–250 / 250–500 / 500–1000 / >1000. Useful if you want to focus on stocks within a specific price range for portfolio sizing.

Sort control

The Sort By dropdown lets you sort by any metric: Score, % Change, Volume, Price, RSI, or Company Name. The ↓ / ↑ button toggles direction. The ↺ Reset button clears all filters at once.

Loading speed

The first time you open the app, it downloads all bhavcopy files (can take 20–30 seconds). Every subsequent visit loads from a local cache — only new files you've added since your last visit are downloaded. This makes subsequent loads much faster — typically just a few seconds.
